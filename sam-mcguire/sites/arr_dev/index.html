Arrested Development is an American sitcom created by Mitchell Hurwitz. As of now, there are four seasons, and the fifth season is currently in production. The first three seasons were aired on Fox from November 2, 2003 to February 10, 2006. The fourth season was released on Netflix on May 26, 2013. The show follows the Bluth family, a formerly wealthy and extremely dysfunctional family. The show is presented in a continuous format, which incorporates hand-held camera work and voice-over narration. It also includes occasional archival photos and historical footage. The show is set in Newport Beach, California, however it was filmed primarily in Culver City and Marina del Rey.

The series has been highly acclaimed, receiving six Primetime Emmy awards and a Golden Globe. It was also ranked sixteenth on Entertainment Weekly's "New TV Classics" list, as well as being ranked in Time magazine's "All-Time 100 TV Shows." However, even despite the acclaim from critics, the sitcom suffered low ratings and was canceled in 2006. Netflix soon agreed to license new episodes and distribute them exclusively.
