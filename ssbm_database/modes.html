1-P Mode

Regular Match

Classic Mode

In this mode, players battle a set of randomly selected opponents. There are three bonus rounds in this mode: "Break the Targets", "Snag the Trophies", and "Race to the Finish". The game ends with a battle against Master Hand.


Adventure Mode

In this mode, players must traverse long stages based on various Nintendo franchises. Certain levels contain enemies, while others consist of one-on-one battles against a character. The game ends with a battle against a larger Bowser.


All-Star Mode

Once all characters are unlocked, this mode can be accessed. Players will be pitted against every character in the game, with only one life and limited health recovery. The game ends with a match against Mr. Game & Watch, in addition to a number of his clones.


Event Match

Event Matches are pre-determined challenges that the player must complete. While only a limited number of matches can be played from the start, more are unlocked as the player progresses. Variations include character and time limitations. The event list is segmented by "All-Star Matches", in which players will battle a set of opponents based on different themes. There are a total of 51 events.


Stadium

Target Test

Derived form the 'Break the Targets' mini-game in Classic Mode. In this mode, players will break a set of 10 targets on stages designed around the mechanics of each individual character.


Home-Run Contest

In this mode, players must use a Home-Run Bat to knock the Sandbag as far as they can within 10 seconds. It is advised to rack up a large amount of damage on Sandbag before knocking it away with the bat. Be careful not to knock Sandbag off the platform.


Multi-Man Melee

In this mode, players must fight against a horde of Fighting Wire-Frames. The player can choose to limit the amount of opponents in 10-Man or 100-Man matches, set a time limit of 3 or 15 minutes, or fight an endless horde of opponents on a normal or "Cruel" difficulty.


Training

Players can hone their skills on AI, learn the layout of a stage, and get a feel for the items in this mode. Unlike in other game modes, the player can manipulate gameplay elements at will. Items may be spawned, the camera position can be altered, AI behavior can be adjusted, and and the speed of the game can be changed.


VS. Mode

Melee

This is the "default" multiplayer mode. Play with up to 4 players in a free-for-all match. You can also change the rules to fight in a stock match, grab the highest amount of coins, or compete for bonus points.


Tournament Melee

Here, you can play in a tournament with up to 64 combatants. The number of players per match can be altered in order to lengthen or shorten the tournament. Players can choose characters and assign names for themselves before the tournament begins.


Special Melee

Camera Mode

In this mode, up to 3 players can fight, while the fourth controller slot can be used as a camera to take screenshots.


Stamina Mode

The standard damage percentage mechanic is replaced with a health meter in this mode.


Super Sudden Death

All matches in this mode crank all players' damage percentages up to 300%.


Giant Melee

Character size is doubled in this mode.


Tiny Melee

Character size is halved in this mode.


Invisible Melee

Players will not be able to see each other in this mode.


Fixed-Camera Mode

In this mode, players get a full view of the stage they're playing on. The camera is static, and will not change to accomodate the location of the players.


Single-Button Mode

Only the control stick and the A button are allowed in this mode.


Lightning Melee

The game plays twice as fast in this mode.


Slo-Mo Melee

The game plays at half-speed in this mode.
